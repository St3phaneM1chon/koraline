# Insurance Council of British Columbia - Getting a Licence

**Source:** https://www.insurancecouncilofbc.com/getting-a-licence/
**Scraped:** 2026-03-24
**Regulator:** Insurance Council of British Columbia (ICBC)

---

## Overview

The Insurance Council of British Columbia licenses and regulates insurance agents and adjusters in the province. All individuals must hold a licence before conducting insurance activities in BC, even if they have submitted an application.

## Classes of Insurance Licence

### General Insurance
- Salesperson Level 1
- Agent Level 2
- Agent Level 3
- Trainee registration
- Available for individuals, corporations, or dealerships

### Life Insurance
- For individuals, partnerships, or corporations

### Accident and Sickness Insurance
- For individuals, partnerships, or corporations

### Insurance Adjusting
- Level 1, Level 2, Level 3
- For individuals, partnerships, or corporations

### Travel Insurance
- For sole-proprietors, partnerships, or corporations

## Application Process

### Individual Licence (Online)
1. Create portal account or login (enter legal name as on government-issued photo ID)
2. Add residential and service address
3. Select "Apply for a Licence" from main menu
4. Complete all required information and upload documents (government ID, qualifying course results)
5. Agency/firm contacted to verify authority to represent
6. Application reviewed by Licensing Officer (typically within 10 business days)
7. Confirmation email on approval

### Trainee Registration (Online)
1. Create account on Insurance Council portal
2. Select "Apply for trainee registration"
3. Confirm Trainee Registration Application type
4. Complete all questions and upload documents (government ID, ICBC Autoplan Basics certificate)
5. Look up employing Agency
6. Agency verification
7. Review within 10-15 business days
8. Confirmation email on approval

### Corporate Licence (Online)
1. Login to portal account
2. Register New Agency/Firm if applicable
3. Governance Stakeholder Application for unlicensed Directors, Officers, Partners, Controlling Shareholders
4. Choose licence class and complete application
5. Review by Licensing Analyst (10-15 business days)
6. Confirmation email on approval

### Apply by Email
- Fill out License Application Form
- Email to licensing@insurancecouncilofbc.com
- Portal account created, payment instructions sent
- Review within 10-15 business days

## Suitability to Hold a Licence
- Criminal record checks required
- Assessment of character and fitness
- First-time applicants, former licensees reinstating, and non-BC residents

## Processing Times
- Standard: 10-15 business days after complete application
- Applications expire 90 days after submission
- Incomplete applications may be closed after minimum two notification attempts

## Licence Fees and Renewal
- Government fee: $25.00 per year
- Licence renewal dates based on issue date
- Licences issued on or before Feb 28, 2026: first renewal June 1, 2026
- Licences issued March 1 - May 31, 2026: first renewal June 1, 2027 (two government fees apply: $50 total)

## Key Policies
- Must hold licence before conducting any insurance activities
- Licensure and other employment/business activities reviewed
- Equivalency-based licensing available from other jurisdictions
