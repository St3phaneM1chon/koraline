# OSFI Guideline B-3 - Sound Reinsurance Practices and Procedures (2025)

**Source:** https://www.osfi-bsif.gc.ca/en/guidance/guidance-library/sound-reinsurance-practices-procedures-guideline-2025
**Scraped:** 2026-03-24
**Regulator:** Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI)
**Category:** Sound Business and Financial Practices
**Effective:** January 1, 2025
**Sectors:** Life Insurance and Fraternal Companies, Property and Casualty Companies

---

## Overview

Reinsurance is an important risk management tool that can be used by an insurer to reduce insurance risks and the volatility of financial results, stabilize solvency, make more efficient use of capital, better withstand catastrophic events, increase underwriting capacity, and to draw on reinsurers' expertise. However, reinsurance exposes an insurer to operational, legal, counterparty, and liquidity risks. Inadequate reinsurance risk management practices can materially affect an insurer's financial soundness and reputation, and can ultimately contribute to its failure.

This guideline applies to all federally regulated insurers (FRIs) that are party to reinsurance cessions, retrocessions and, where applicable, to assumption reinsurance transactions.

## I. Key Principles

### Principle 1: Sound Reinsurance Risk Management Policy (RRMP)
A FRI should have a sound and comprehensive RRMP that is overseen by senior management. The RRMP should form an integral component of its overall enterprise-wide risk management framework and reflect the scale, nature and complexity of business.

Key RRMP elements:
- Purpose and objectives for seeking reinsurance
- Ceding limits
- Counterparty risk assessment
- Concentration limits
- Liquidity risk processes

**Ceding limits**: A FRI should not, in normal course, cede 100% or substantially all of its insurance risks.

**Counterparty risk**: A FRI should monitor multiple factors. Counterparty risk should be considered at aggregate level for stress testing, from both going-concern and gone-concern perspectives.

**Senior Management Oversight**: Senior management should oversee development, implementation, and operationalization of the RRMP. Minimum annual review required.

### Principle 2: Due Diligence on Counterparties
A FRI should evaluate the ability of all current and prospective reinsurance counterparties to meet their liabilities under severe but plausible adverse events on an ongoing basis. Evaluation should consider:
- Claims payment record
- Expected future claims obligations
- Balance sheet strength
- Funding sources, capital, and liquidity
- Management and governance quality
- Retrocession arrangements

Due diligence should not rely solely on third parties (rating agencies, broker analysis). Higher due diligence expected for unregistered reinsurers.

### Principle 3: Clarity and Certainty of Reinsurance Contracts
Comprehensive, written, binding reinsurance contracts should be executed prior to the effective date. Summary documents should include premium/consideration, risk percentages, risks reinsured, duration, exclusions, and standard clauses.

### Principle 4: Contracts Should Not Adversely Affect the Ceding FRI
- Insolvency clause required in all contracts
- Reinsurance receivables must be paid directly to the FRI-cedant in Canada
- No offset/cut-through clauses that prejudice policyholders
- Contracts subject to laws and courts of Canada or equivalent jurisdiction

## II. Guideline Administration

A FRI should maintain and provide to OSFI upon request: its RRMP, descriptions of all reinsurance arrangements, due diligence performed, and stress testing results.

Non-adherence to principles may result in OSFI not granting capital credit for the reinsurance arrangement or adjusting capital requirements.
