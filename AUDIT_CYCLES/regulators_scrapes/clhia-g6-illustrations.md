# CLHIA Guideline G6 - Illustrations

**Source:** https://www.clhia.ca/en-CA/Industry/clhia-guidelines/g06-illustrations
**Scraped:** 2026-03-24
**Organization:** Canadian Life and Health Insurance Association (CLHIA)
**Date:** April 28, 2009
**Category:** Distribution, Product Disclosure, Registered Products (RRSP, LIRA, RRIF)

---

## Purpose

This Guideline recommends practices for the preparation and distribution of illustrations, both at point-of-sale and for in-force policies.

## Scope

The Guideline sets out practices for the preparation and distribution of illustrations. More specifically, the Guideline calls for:

1. **Guaranteed vs. Non-Guaranteed Disclosure**: Clearly disclosing whether values or features in the policy are guaranteed or not guaranteed.

2. **Non-Guaranteed Value Variability**: If any values or features are not guaranteed, disclosing how and under what circumstances the non-guaranteed values may vary and what the effect may be on the policy.

## Key Requirements

### Point-of-Sale Illustrations
- Must clearly distinguish between guaranteed and non-guaranteed values
- Assumptions regarding loan rates should be consistent
- Surrender charges should be indicated or net values illustrated
- Illustrations are not intended to be predictive
- Reasonable measures should be taken to ensure illustration is based on sound assumptions

### In-Force Policy Illustrations
- Updated illustrations should reflect current policy status
- Non-guaranteed elements should be clearly identified
- Changes from original illustration assumptions should be noted

### General Standards
- Illustrations should not be misleading
- Standardized presentation formats recommended
- Assumptions should be clearly stated
- Both optimistic and conservative scenarios should be presented where appropriate
