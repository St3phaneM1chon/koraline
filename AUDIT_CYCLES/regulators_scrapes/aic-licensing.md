# Alberta Insurance Council (AIC) - Licensing Requirements

**Source:** https://www.abcouncil.ab.ca/applying-for-a-license/
**Scraped:** 2026-03-24
**Regulator:** Alberta Insurance Council (AIC)

---

## Overview

Providing insurance services is a regulated occupation in Alberta. Both individuals and businesses must be licensed by AIC to sell, adjust, or otherwise provide insurance-related services. An individual must have their license before providing insurance-related services, even if they have finished their education and have an employment contract.

**Standard application review time: 15 business days**

## License Types

### General Insurance Licenses
- Three levels (1, 2, 3) plus probationary option
- Course equivalencies accepted (CAIB or CIP designations)

### Life Insurance Licenses
- Represent an agency
- Sole proprietor / Sole proprietor with trade name
- Employee of an insurance company

### Accident & Sickness Insurance Licenses
- Same structure as Life Insurance licenses

### Adjuster Licenses

### Business Licenses
- Designated Representative licensing for businesses
- Restricted Business licenses

## Certification Requirements

### 1. CIPR Number (Canadian Insurance Participant Registry)
- Unique identifier used across all jurisdictions in Canada
- Register at cipr-rcpa.com
- Required before applying

### 2. Education and Exam Completion
- Education requirements vary by license class and level
- Must pass required provincial exam
- **Exam marks valid for 12 months only**
- AIC must receive paid and approved applications within 12 months of passing exam
- Exemptions:
  - Re-applying within 24 months of last active license: exempt from rewriting exam
  - Currently licensed in equivalent class in another jurisdiction: exempt from exam

### 3. Criminal Record Check
- From MyBackCheck or local police/RCMP
- **Must be issued within last 3 months** from date AIC receives application
- AIC does NOT accept third-party checks from Triton, Certn, or Verifications Canada
- U.S. residents: FBI or First Advantage check required

### 4. Errors & Omissions (E&O) Insurance
- Active policy meeting AIC-approved terms and conditions
- Must provide: carrier name, policy number, effective/expiry dates
- Insurer employees exempt from this requirement

### 5. Employer/Agency Sponsorship
- **Mandatory** - Must have employer (agency or insurance company) to recommend licenses
- Required both to submit application and to hold a license

### 6. Conflicts of Interest
- Must not be in position to use coercion or undue influence
- Must not be engaged in occupation creating conflict of interest

## Exam Programs

### General License Qualification Program (GLQP)
- For general insurance (P&C) licenses

### Life License Qualification Program (LLQP)
- For life insurance licenses
- National program, recognized across provinces

## Continuing Education
- Required for license renewal
- Failure to complete by June 30 causes automatic license expiration
- Courses accredited by Alberta Accreditation Committee
- Non-Alberta residents in certain jurisdictions may be exempt

## Insurance Act Definitions

### Insurance Agent (Section 1(bb))
A person who for compensation:
- Solicits insurance on behalf of insurer/insured
- Transmits applications for insurance
- Transmits policies of insurance
- Negotiates or offers to negotiate insurance
- Enrolls individuals in prescribed group insurance contracts

### Adjuster (Section 2(1))
A person who for compensation solicits the right to negotiate or negotiates settlement of loss claims under insurance contracts.

## Non-Alberta Residents
May be exempt from qualifying exams if holding equivalent license in another province. Must still submit applications and meet Alberta licensing requirements (not automatically transferred).
