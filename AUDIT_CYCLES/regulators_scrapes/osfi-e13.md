# OSFI Guideline E-13 - Regulatory Compliance Management (RCM) (2014)

**Source:** https://www.osfi-bsif.gc.ca/en/guidance/guidance-library/regulatory-compliance-management-rcm-guideline-2014
**Scraped:** 2026-03-24
**Regulator:** Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI)
**Category:** Sound Business and Financial Practices
**Sectors:** Banks, Foreign Bank Branches, Foreign Insurance Branches, Life Insurance and Fraternal Companies, Property and Casualty Companies, Trust and Loan Companies

---

## I. Purpose and Scope of the Guideline

The purpose of the RCM guideline is to communicate OSFI's expectations with respect to the management of regulatory compliance risk by federally regulated financial institutions (FRFIs).

This guideline revises and replaces the 2003 LCM Guideline to better align it with guidance provided by more recently updated OSFI Guidelines.

The guideline also elaborates on a number of principles regarding key controls as part of RCM.

OSFI recognizes that FRFIs may have different RCM practices depending on a variety of factors, including their: size; ownership structure; nature, scope and complexity of operations; corporate strategy; risk profile; and geographical locations.

## II. Definitions

### (i) Regulatory Compliance Management (RCM)
The term "Regulatory Compliance Management" (RCM) in this guideline refers to the set of key controls through which a FRFI manages regulatory compliance risk.

### (ii) Regulatory Compliance Risk
Regulatory compliance risk is the risk of a FRFI's potential non-conformance with laws, rules, regulations and prescribed practices ("regulatory requirements") in any jurisdiction in which it operates. It does not include risk arising from non-conformance with ethical standards.

### (iii) RCM Framework
A RCM framework refers to the structures, processes and other key control elements through which a FRFI and its subsidiaries manage and mitigate regulatory compliance risk inherent in their activities enterprise-wide.

## III. RCM Framework - Overview

OSFI considers an effective RCM framework to be an essential component of an overall risk management program. Non-compliance with applicable regulatory requirements can have significant negative effects on a FRFI's reputation and/or safety and soundness and may lead to increased regulatory intervention.

The RCM framework should enable a FRFI to apply a risk-based approach for identifying, risk-assessing, communicating, managing and mitigating regulatory compliance risk.

Overall responsibility for assessment and management of regulatory compliance risk within the FRFI should be assigned to an individual who is independent from operational management -- designated as the FRFI's Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) or equivalent.

OSFI assesses the quality of RCM at the following levels of control:
- Operational management for a given business activity (day-to-day controls)
- Independent, enterprise-wide oversight of operational management by oversight functions

OSFI expects the RCM framework to be reviewed and updated regularly, at least annually.

## IV. RCM Framework Key Controls

### (i) Role of the CCO
The CCO should be responsible for assessing the adequacy of, adherence to and effectiveness of the FRFI's day-to-day controls. The CCO should have a clearly defined and documented mandate, unfettered access, and a direct reporting line to the Board or Branch Management.

### (ii) Procedures for Identifying and Managing Regulatory Compliance Risk
Reasonable procedures should exist to assure that appropriate individuals are provided with current and accurate information needed to identify, assess, communicate, manage and mitigate regulatory compliance risk.

### (iii) Day-to-Day Compliance Procedures
Appropriate procedures should exist in operational management to reasonably assure that a FRFI is complying on a day-to-day basis with applicable regulatory requirements.

### (iv) Independent Monitoring and Testing Procedures
Day-to-day compliance procedures should be independently overseen by the CCO using a risk-based approach. The adequacy of compliance oversight should be validated by Internal Audit or other independent review function.

### (v) Internal Reporting
- **Reporting Procedures**: Sufficient pertinent information about RCM should be communicated on a timely basis to Senior Management.
- **Compliance Reports to Senior Management**: Regular reports should allow clear understanding of regulatory risks and adequacy of key controls.
- **Internal Audit Reports**: Should include scope and results of RCM-related audits.

### (vi) Role of Internal Audit
The CCO's activities should be subject to periodic review by Internal Audit. The scope should consider the reliability of the RCM framework.

### (vii) Adequate Documentation
Roles and responsibilities of all individuals involved in RCM should be clearly documented. Both day-to-day and independent oversight levels should produce sufficient documentation.

### (viii) Role of Senior Management
Senior Management should oversee the RCM framework and take reasonable measures to assure that compliance policies are adequate, regularly reviewed, and that all staff understand their responsibilities.

## V. OSFI's Supervisory Assessment

OSFI conducts supervisory work and monitors the performance of FRFIs to assess safety and soundness, the quality of control and governance processes, and regulatory compliance. OSFI's assessment focuses on the FRFI's ability to manage its regulatory compliance risk.
