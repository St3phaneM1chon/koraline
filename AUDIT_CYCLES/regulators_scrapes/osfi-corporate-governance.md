# OSFI Corporate Governance Guideline (2018)

**Source:** https://www.osfi-bsif.gc.ca/en/guidance/guidance-library/corporate-governance-guideline-2018
**Scraped:** 2026-03-24
**Regulator:** Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI)
**Category:** Sound Business and Financial Practices
**Effective:** September 30, 2018
**Sectors:** Banks, Life Insurance and Fraternal Companies, Property and Casualty Companies, Trust and Loan Companies

---

## I. Purpose and Scope

This guideline communicates OSFI's expectations with respect to corporate governance of federally regulated financial institutions (FRFIs). It applies to all FRFIs other than branch operations of foreign banks and foreign insurance companies.

OSFI's expectations are principles-based and recognize that practices may depend on size, ownership structure, nature/scope/complexity of operations, strategy, and risk profile.

## II. The Board of Directors

### Role of the Board
The Board is responsible for the FRFI's business plan, strategy, and risk appetite and culture. Essential duties include:

**Approve and oversee:**
- Short-term and long-term business plan and strategy
- Risk Appetite Framework and Internal Control Framework
- Significant capital and liquidity policies
- Codes of ethics and conduct
- Appointment, performance review, and compensation of CEO and Senior Management
- Succession plans
- External and internal audit plans

**Provide challenge, advice and guidance on:**
- Significant operational, business, risk and crisis management policies
- Compensation policy consistent with FSB Principles for Sound Compensation
- Business performance relative to Board-approved plans
- Effectiveness of Risk Appetite Framework, Internal Control Framework, and Oversight Functions

### The Board and Senior Management
Senior Management is composed of the CEO and individuals directly accountable to the CEO (CFO, CRO, CCO, CIA, CA, and business unit heads). They implement the Board's decisions and direct operations.

### The Board and Oversight Functions
Oversight Functions provide objective assessments and include Financial, Risk Management, Compliance, Internal Audit, and Actuarial functions. Heads should have sufficient stature, authority, independence, and unfettered access to the Board.

### Board Effectiveness
- Board should be diverse with balance of expertise, skills, experience
- Board and Chair should be independent from Senior Management
- Separation of Chair and CEO is critical
- Regular self-assessment of practices

## III. Risk Governance

### Risk Appetite Framework
- Enterprise-wide, reflecting risk profile
- Contains risk appetite statement (qualitative + quantitative), risk limits
- Should be forward-looking, consider normal and stressed scenarios
- CRO ensures aggregate limits are consistent with risk appetite statement

### Board Risk Committee
- Oversees risk management enterprise-wide
- All members should be non-executive
- Receives timely reports on significant risks and exposures

### Chief Risk Officer (CRO)
- Responsible for oversight of all risks across the firm
- Independent from operational management
- Unfettered access and functional reporting line to Board
- Compensation not linked to business line performance
- Reports regularly to Board, Risk Committee, and Senior Management

## IV. The Role of the Audit Committee
- Required by federal legislation, comprised of non-employee directors
- Reviews annual statements, evaluates internal control procedures
- Approves internal and external audit plans
- Recommends external auditor appointment to shareholders
- Should meet with external auditor and CIA with and without CEO present

## V. Supervision of FRFIs
OSFI assesses quality of oversight and control provided by Board and Senior Management. Open communication between Board and regulators is expected. FRFIs should notify OSFI of potential changes to Board/Senior Management and provide candidate qualifications.

## Annex B - Risk Appetite Framework

### Risk Appetite Statement
- Reflects aggregate level and type of risk the FRFI is willing to accept
- Linked to strategic, capital and financial plans, and compensation programs
- Includes qualitative measures (risks wanted/avoided, attitudes) and quantitative measures (loss, capital, liquidity measures)
- Should be forward-looking, consider normal and stressed scenarios

### Risk Limits
Allocation of risk appetite to specific risk categories, business units, lines of business, and more granular levels. Often expressed in quantitative terms, specific, measurable, frequency-based and reportable.
