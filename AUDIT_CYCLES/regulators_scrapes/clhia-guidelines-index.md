## Overview

The CLHIA is a not-for-profit, membership-based organization that represents 99% of Canada's life and health insurance companies. CLHIA Guidelines are designed to promote consistent practices and standards for the life and health insurance industry and to reinforce the best interests of consumers and the industry.

**Note:** The CLHIA has migrated from the old "LD" numbering system to "G" prefixed guidelines. The formerly referenced LD2, LD7, LD10 correspond to the current G-series guidelines.

## Key Guidelines for Insurance Education

### G1 - Product Disclosure (formerly related to LD series)
Sets out practices for printed disclosure (hard copy or electronic) by life and health insurance companies about their products to promote informed consumer decisions.

### G6 - Illustrations (key for LLQP/insurance education)
Recommends practices for preparation and distribution of illustrations at point-of-sale and for in-force policies. Key requirement: clearly disclose whether values or features are guaranteed or non-guaranteed, and how non-guaranteed values may vary.

### G8 - Advisor Suitability
Screening, monitoring and reporting standards for advisors representing insurance companies.

### G14 - Confirming Advisor Disclosure
Practices to confirm advisors make disclosure consistent with industry Reference Document on Advisor Disclosure.
